## RULE BUNCH II — HOW TO DO THE FIGHTS (GENERAL STUFF)

**Part 2.01. Don't Mess With the Spawn, Stupid.** You are NOT allowed to go into the red zones where people spawn. You can't walk in there. You can't shoot in there. You can't throw stuff in there. You can't even shoot NEAR there or KINDA TOWARD there. The spawn zone is like a special place, like a bathroom — you don't bother people in there. BUT if some jackass is shooting at you FROM the spawn zone, then yeah, you can shoot back, 'cause that guy's being a dick and he knows it.

**Part 2.02. Your Vehicle Gotta Work With LBA.** All your tanks and mechs and hover-thingies and power armor and whatever gotta be compatible with LBA (which stands for Listen Based Armor, and no we don't know why it's called that, that's just what it's called, stop asking). If it don't work with LBA, you can't use it. It's that simple. Even a PLANT could understand this.

**Part 2.03. You Gotta Be THIS Tall to Fight.** Your avatar gotta be at LEAST one and a half meters tall. That's like... up to here. *[gestures vaguely]* We measured it from the bottom of your feet to the top of your head. If you're shorter than that, you can't fight. Go play somewhere else, short stuff. Maybe drink some Brawndo — it's got what plants crave, and maybe it'll make you taller. Probably not though.

**Part 2.04. Keep Your Pre-Jump On.** Your pre-jump thing has to be turned on. ALL the time. Don't turn it off. Don't hack it. Don't "accidentally" have it not working. We WILL notice and we WILL be mad about it.

**Part 2.05. Shut Up.** Don't be loud. If the Bosses think you're being too loud or annoying or making sounds that hurt our ears or make us want to punch you in the face, that's not allowed. What counts as "too loud" is whatever WE say is too loud because we're the Bosses and also our heads hurt.

## RULE BUNCH III — GUN RULES (THE IMPORTANT PART)

**Part 3.01. No Cheaty Aim Guns.** You can't use guns that find people automatically using sensors or the agent list or whatever robot-targety thing. That's cheating and cheating is for people who eat at places that aren't Carl's Jr.

**Part 3.02. You Gotta Run Outta Bullets.** Your gun has to run out of ammo eventually. You can't have infinite bullets. That goes for turrets too and those little robot shooty things you put on the ground. Everything runs out. That's just science.

**Part 3.03. No Shooting Through Walls.** If there's a wall between you and the other guy, your bullets gotta stop at the wall. That's what walls are FOR. We shouldn't have to explain this but here we are because some of y'all are, and I say this with all due respect, dumb as hell.

**Part 3.04. Raycast Gun Rules.** Full-auto raycast guns? BANNED. Gone. Get 'em outta here. Burst-fire raycast guns are okay UNLESS they make that weird haze effect that makes everything look like you're trying to fight inside a fart cloud. No haze. Nobody likes haze. Haze is stupid.

**Part 3.05. Shotgun Rules.** Shotguns gotta shoot either actual physical pellet thingies or use raycast. That's it. Those are your two choices. It's like the menu at Carl's Jr. — pick one and stop holding up the line.

**Part 3.06. Bullets Don't Go Through Walls Either (Yes We're Saying It Again).** Bullets and projectiles and whatever you're shooting DO NOT go through walls, barricades, shields, or any other thing that's supposed to stop bullets. We already said this in 3.03 but some of you need to hear things twice. Or three times. Maybe with pictures. We're looking into getting pictures.

### Part B — Explosions and Boom-Boom Stuff

**Part 3.07. How Big Your Boom Can Be.** Your explosive stuff can only kill people within 5 meters and only hurt people within 10 meters. That's the maximum bigness of your boom. HOWEVER if your explosive goes in mostly one direction (like a skinny boom) the Bosses MIGHT let it slide. We'll decide. Don't ask us when. We'll get to it. We're busy.

**Part 3.08. Extra Big Booms Need a Warning.** If your explosive is bigger than what Part 3.07 says, you can ONLY use it if it has a big obvious wind-up thing — like it makes a noise AND does a visual thing for a few seconds so people can go "OH CRAP" and run away. Basically you gotta give 'em a chance. It's called sportsmanship, look it up.

**Part 3.09. Gotta Be Shootable.** If your explosive/missile/bomb/whatever has a kill zone bigger than 2 meters, people gotta be able to shoot it down. No teleporting missiles. No magic disappearing rockets. No "it was there and then it wasn't and then you're dead." If it flies, it can die. That's the law. Well, it's a rule. Same thing basically.

**Part 3.10. Explosions Don't Go Through Walls (Are You Sensing a Pattern Here?).** Your bombs can't kill people through walls. If there's a wall between the boom and the guy, the guy lives. That's just how walls work. We believe in walls here at Ashguard.

**Part 3.11. How Big Your Mines Gotta Be.** Land mines and proximity bombs and stuff have to be at least 0.25 by 0.25 by 0.04 meters. That's so people can actually SEE them. Because invisible mines are some bush-league nonsense and we don't tolerate bush-league nonsense. Not on OUR territory.

**Part 3.12. No Group-Safe Explosions.** Your explosions gotta hurt EVERYBODY equally. Your bomb don't care if someone's on your team — BOOM, everyone gets boomed. That's democracy. That's how we do it. If you don't like it, there's the door.

## RULE BUNCH IV — MOVING AROUND AND STUFF

### Part A — Jetpack Thingies

**Part 4.01.** Your jetpack can't recharge while you're in the air. You gotta land first. On the GROUND. With your FEET. Like a person. You wanna recharge? Touch grass. Literally.

**Part 4.02.** After you use your jetpack, you gotta wait at LEAST 3 seconds before you can use it again. Three. Tres. That many. *[holds up three fingers]*

**Part 4.03.** Your jetpack can't fly you TOO far. What's "too far"? Well, if you can cross the WHOLE region in one go, that's definitely too far. The Bosses will know it when they see it. We got good eyes. Well, decent eyes. We got eyes.

### Part B — Runnin' Fast (Sprint)

**Part 4.04.** You can sprint for a maximum of 5 seconds at a time. Then you gotta stop sprinting. 'Cause your avatar is tired. Even though avatars don't actually get tired. It's the principle of the thing. Don't question it.

**Part 4.05.** After you sprint, you gotta wait at LEAST 6 seconds before you sprint again. That's the cooldown. Like when you eat too many jalapeno poppers from Carl's Jr. and you gotta sit for a minute.

### Part C — Diverolls and Dashy Things

**Part 4.06.** Your diveroll or dash can't move you more than 15 meters. Fifteen. That's it. If you're diverolling further than that, you're not diverolling, you're TELEPORTING, and that's not what this section is about.

**Part 4.07.** You can't dash or diveroll in the AIR. You gotta be on the ground. This is a GROUND move. It's right there in the name. Well, it's not, but it should be. We'll workshop it.

**Part 4.08.** Gotta wait 4 seconds between dashes/diverolls. Four seconds. Count 'em. One... two... three... four. See? That wasn't so hard. Now do that every time.

## RULE BUNCH V — STUFF YOU WEAR AND CARRY (PERSONAL JUNK)

**Part 5.01. Riot Shields (The Hidey Things).** You can use a riot shield but it can only have 50 HP max, and it only protects you from the direction it's facing. It's a shield, not a force field. You want a force field, go to a different sim. Or the future. Whichever comes first.

**Part 5.02. Blindy Stuff and Leg-Stoppy Things.** If you got equipment that blinds people or slows 'em down or whatever, the recharge time has to be LONGER than how long the effect lasts. So if you blind someone for 3 seconds, you gotta wait MORE than 3 seconds to do it again. Math. We know it's hard.

**Part 5.03. Body Armor (Wearing Protection).** If you wear armor that covers you all the way around (360 degrees), it can only block 25% of damage. One quarter. That's it. You want more protection? Carry a shield too. Or hide behind a wall. We already established that walls work great around here.

**Part 5.04. Healing and Getting Un-Hurt.** If you got something that heals you, it either has a limited number of uses per life, OR it takes a really long time to recharge — long enough that you can't just stand there healing forever like some kind of immortal jackass. Pick one. Or both. We're not picky. Actually wait, yes we are.

## RULE BUNCH VI — STUFF YOU PUT ON THE GROUND (DEPLOYABLES)

**Part 6.01. Explodey Things You Put Down.** Deployable bombs, claymores, and remote-detonation thingies can only have 1 HP. ONE. That means literally any bullet can destroy them. A harsh look could probably destroy them. That's the point. Deal with it.

**Part 6.02. Buildings and Walls You Put Down.** When you first put down a barricade or fort or whatever, it can have up to 100 HP. You can fix it up and make it stronger, but it can NEVER go above 400 HP. Four hundred. That's the ceiling. The absolute maximum. The "this far and no further." Four. Hundred.

### Part B — Robot Gun Turrets

**Part 6.03. You Only Get One.** Each person gets ONE turret. Uno. Ein. One. ONE. Not two. Not three. One. If you could use more fingers to count your turrets, you got too many turrets.

**Part 6.04. It Takes a Minute.** Your turret can't just start shooting the SECOND you put it down. It needs a build time. A construction phase. A little "please wait" moment. Like waiting for your Gentleman's Latte at Starbucks. These things take time.

**Part 6.05. It Can't See Everything.** Your turret can only see and shoot in a 180-degree arc. That's half a circle. A semicircle if you went to college. Which, statistically, you probably didn't, and that's okay. We love you anyway. Welcome to Costco.

**Part 6.06. Turret Boom-Boom Ammo.** Turrets CAN use explosive ammo but ONLY if the turret has to lock on first before shooting. No lock-on, no explosives. That's the deal. Take it or leave it. Actually don't leave it, just take it, it's a rule, you don't get a choice.
