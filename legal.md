# ASHGUARD COMBAT REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

---

**Comprehensive Operational Code Governing the Conduct of Armed Engagements,
Permissible Armaments, Vehicular Deployments, and Ancillary Equipment
Within the Territorial Jurisdiction of Ashguard**

---

*Promulgated and Ratified by the Ashguard Governing Authority*
*Effective Upon Publication — All Prior Versions Hereby Superseded*

</div>

---

## PREAMBLE

*WHEREAS, the Ashguard Governing Authority (hereinafter referred to as "the Authority") deems it necessary and expedient to establish a comprehensive regulatory framework governing all forms of armed engagement, the deployment of weaponry, vehicular operations, avatar modifications, and ancillary equipment within the territorial boundaries of Ashguard;*

*WHEREAS, the Authority reserves unto itself the plenary and non-derogable right to direct any participant to cease and desist from the use of any equipment, armament, system, or tactical methodology not expressly enumerated or addressed within the provisions hereinbelow, pending formal adjudicative review and determination by the Authority;*

*WHEREAS, notwithstanding any provision to the contrary contained herein, deviations from the stipulations of this Code may be permitted where and only where all affected parties to the engagement have provided express, informed, and unequivocal mutual consent thereto;*

*NOW, THEREFORE, the following Articles, Sections, and Subsections shall constitute the binding and enforceable rules of engagement within the jurisdiction of Ashguard:*

---

## ARTICLE I — SCHEDULE OF PROHIBITED EQUIPMENT

**Section 1.01.** The following classes of armaments and equipment are hereby declared *per se* prohibited within the jurisdiction of Ashguard. Possession, deployment, discharge, or any attempt to utilize the items enumerated in this Section shall constitute a material breach of this Code and shall subject the offending party to such sanctions, penalties, or remedial measures as the Authority may, in its sole and absolute discretion, impose:

> (a) All weapons, armaments, munitions, and derivative systems manufactured, distributed, or bearing the designation of the SAC organization, without exception or limitation as to model, variant, caliber, or configuration;
>
> (b) The specific armament designated as the "Chaos Decretum Exodus Rifle," including all variants, modifications, derivatives, and functionally equivalent reproductions thereof.

---

## ARTICLE II — GENERAL RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

**Section 2.01. Spawn Zone Sanctity.** No combatant shall, under any circumstances, ingress into, trespass upon, or otherwise physically enter any zone demarcated in red and designated as a spawn area. Furthermore, no combatant shall discharge any weapon, munition, projectile, or area-of-effect ordnance into, toward, or in the general direction of such spawn zones; *provided, however*, that defensive return fire directed toward a hostile combatant who has initiated an attack from within or proximate to a spawn zone shall constitute a permissible exception to this prohibition.

**Section 2.02. Armor System Compatibility.** All vehicles, mechanized units, powered armor, and any system incorporating damage mitigation or hit-point allocation functionality shall be required, as a condition precedent to deployment, to maintain full and verified compatibility with the LBA (Listen Based Armor) protocol. Non-compliant systems are strictly prohibited.

**Section 2.03. Minimum Avatar Dimensional Requirements.** No avatar shall be permitted to engage in combat operations unless said avatar meets or exceeds a minimum vertical dimension of one and one-half meters (1.5 m) as measured from the base of the avatar to its highest anatomical point. Avatars failing to satisfy this dimensional threshold are hereby barred from participation in any and all combat activities.

**Section 2.04. Pre-Jump Enablement.** All participants shall, at all times during combat operations, maintain their pre-jump functionality in an active and enabled state. The deactivation, circumvention, or suppression of pre-jump capability is expressly prohibited.

**Section 2.05. Acoustic Disturbance Prohibition.** The emission, broadcast, or reproduction of any sound, audio signal, or acoustic output that is, in the reasonable judgment of the Authority, excessively loud, obnoxious, disruptive, or otherwise deleterious to the auditory environment of the combat theater is strictly forbidden.

---

## ARTICLE III — WEAPONS REGULATIONS

**Section 3.01. Sensor and Agent List Weaponry.** The use, deployment, or discharge of any weapon or weapons system that operates by means of sensor detection, agent list enumeration, or any analogous targeting mechanism that derives target acquisition data from the simulator's agent registry is categorically prohibited.

**Section 3.02. Infinite Ammunition Prohibition.** No weapon, ordnance delivery system, or deployable apparatus shall be permitted to operate with an inexhaustible, unlimited, or effectively infinite ammunition supply. This prohibition extends to and encompasses all deployable systems, turrets, and autonomous or semi-autonomous firing platforms.

**Section 3.03. Obstacle Penetration Prohibition.** Any weapon, munition, or projectile specifically designed, engineered, or configured to inflict damage upon or terminate a combatant through obstacles, barriers, walls, or structures that would, under standard operational conditions, be reasonably expected to arrest, deflect, or absorb said projectile is hereby prohibited.

**Section 3.04. Raycast Weapon Restrictions.** Fully automatic raycast weapons—defined as any weapon employing raycast hit-detection methodology operating in a continuous or sustained automatic firing mode—are not permitted under the present regulatory regime. Burst-fire raycast weapons shall be permitted *provided that* said weapons do not produce, emit, or generate any visual obscuration effect commonly referred to as "haze."

**Section 3.05. Shotgun Specifications.** All weapons classified as shotguns shall be required to discharge either physical primitive-based projectiles or utilize raycast-based hit detection. No alternative projectile methodology shall be permitted for this weapon class.

**Section 3.06. Projectile Impermeability.** Bullets, projectiles, and munitions of all types shall not penetrate, pass through, or otherwise traverse walls, barricades, shield implementations, or any defensive structure or apparatus designed and deployed for the purpose of arresting projectile motion.

### Part B — Explosives and Area-of-Effect Ordnance

**Section 3.07. Blast Radius Limitations.** All explosive ordnance, area-of-effect munitions, and detonation-capable devices shall conform to the following maximum effective radii: a lethal kill radius not exceeding five meters (5 m) and a wound/injury radius not exceeding ten meters (10 m); *provided, however*, that highly directional explosive devices (i.e., those whose blast pattern is substantially confined to a narrow cone or arc) may be exempted from the foregoing limitations at the Authority's discretion.

**Section 3.08. Exceeding Standard Blast Parameters.** Explosive ordnance that exceeds the radii prescribed in Section 3.07 shall be permitted only where the detonation is preceded by a conspicuous wind-up period, choreographed through both visual and audible indicators of sufficient duration (not less than several seconds) to afford affected combatants a reasonable opportunity to seek cover or evacuate the blast zone.

**Section 3.09. Interceptability Requirement.** Any explosive device, projectile, missile, or bomb possessing a kill radius in excess of two meters (2 m) shall be interceptable by defensive countermeasures. Explosive projectiles, guided missiles, or aerial munitions that employ non-physical movement, scripted teleportation, or any analogous evasive methodology designed or intended to defeat, circumvent, or render ineffective interceptor systems are expressly prohibited.

**Section 3.10. Explosive Wall Penetration.** No explosive device or area-of-effect ordnance shall inflict lethal or injurious damage upon any combatant situated on the opposing side of a wall, barrier, or solid structural object from the point of detonation.

**Section 3.11. Deployable Mine Specifications.** All deployable land mines, proximity-activated explosive devices, and analogous ground-based ordnance shall conform to minimum physical dimensions of not less than 0.25 meters in length, 0.25 meters in width, and 0.04 meters in height (0.25 × 0.25 × 0.04 m).

**Section 3.12. Group Safety Prohibition for AoE.** Area-of-effect damage and explosive damage systems shall not incorporate, utilize, or be configured with group-safe functionality. All explosive and area-of-effect damage shall apply uniformly and without discrimination as to group affiliation.

## ARTICLE IV — REGULATION OF MOVEMENT ABILITIES

### Part A — Jetpack Systems

**Section 4.01.** Jetpack propulsion systems shall not be capable of recharging, regenerating, or replenishing their fuel, energy, or operational capacity while the avatar is airborne or otherwise not in contact with a solid terrestrial surface.

**Section 4.02.** The minimum recharge interval for jetpack systems, measured from the cessation of thrust to the point at which the system becomes available for subsequent activation, shall be not less than three (3) seconds.

**Section 4.03.** Jetpack systems shall propel the avatar only such distances as are deemed reasonable by the Authority. For purposes of this Section, traversal of the full width of a region in a single jetpack activation shall be deemed *per se* unreasonable.

### Part B — Sprint Systems

**Section 4.04.** Sprint-type movement augmentation systems shall not exceed a maximum continuous activation duration of five (5) seconds per engagement cycle.

**Section 4.05.** The minimum recharge interval between successive sprint activations shall be not less than six (6) seconds.

### Part C — Diverolls and Dash Systems

**Section 4.06.** The maximum lateral or directional displacement achievable through a single diveroll or dash maneuver shall not exceed fifteen meters (15 m).

**Section 4.07.** Dash and diveroll systems shall not be activatable while the avatar is airborne, in freefall, or otherwise not in contact with a solid surface.

**Section 4.08.** The minimum recharge interval between successive dash or diveroll activations shall be not less than four (4) seconds.

---

## ARTICLE V — AVATAR EQUIPMENT AND PERSONAL APPARATUS

**Section 5.01. Riot Shields.** The deployment of riot shields and analogous personal barrier devices is permitted, *subject to* the following conditions: (a) the maximum hit-point allocation of any such shield shall not exceed fifty (50) HP; and (b) the protective coverage afforded by the shield must be directional in nature, i.e., providing protection only from the direction in which the shield is oriented, and shall not provide omnidirectional coverage.

**Section 5.02. Disorientation and Impediment Devices.** Any equipment, system, or device that produces blindness, visual impairment, movement restriction, or any analogous debilitating effect upon an opposing combatant shall be subject to the requirement that the recharge time of said device exceed the duration of the effect imposed. In no case shall the recharge interval be less than or equal to the active effect duration.

**Section 5.03. Body Armor.** Body armor, personal shielding, or any wearable damage mitigation system that affords protection through a full three hundred sixty degrees (360°) of coverage shall not provide a damage reduction factor in excess of twenty-five percent (25%) of incoming damage under the current regulatory dispensation.

**Section 5.04. Healing and Regeneration Systems.** Any equipment or ability that restores, regenerates, or otherwise replenishes the hit points of an avatar shall be subject to one or both of the following constraints: (a) the total number of uses shall be limited on a per-life basis; or (b) the recharge time between successive activations shall be of such extended duration as to prevent continuous or near-continuous regeneration.

## ARTICLE VI — DEPLOYABLE STRUCTURES AND APPARATUS

**Section 6.01. Deployable Explosive Devices.** Deployable explosive devices, including but not limited to explosive charges, claymores, and remotely detonated ordnance, shall be assigned a maximum hit-point value not exceeding one (1) HP and shall be destructible by standard projectiles of any caliber or type.

**Section 6.02. Deployable Structure Hit Points.** All deployable structures, barricades, fortifications, and defensive emplacements shall, at the time of initial deployment, possess a hit-point allocation not exceeding one hundred (100) HP. Subsequent repair, reinforcement, or construction activities may increase the hit-point value of such structures beyond the initial deployment maximum, *provided that* the total hit-point value shall not, under any circumstances, exceed the absolute maximum of four hundred (400) HP.

### Part B — Autonomous Turret Systems

**Section 6.03. Turret Allocation.** Each individual combatant shall be entitled to deploy and operate no more than one (1) autonomous or semi-autonomous turret system at any given time during combat operations.

**Section 6.04. Construction Requirement.** All turret systems shall require a construction phase following initial deployment. No turret shall attain operational status or be capable of discharging ordnance instantaneously upon deployment. A minimum construction interval must elapse before the turret becomes functional.

**Section 6.05. Field of Fire.** The maximum field of view and corresponding field of fire for any autonomous turret system shall not exceed one hundred eighty degrees (180°).

**Section 6.06. Turret Explosive Ammunition.** The utilization of explosive ammunition in autonomous turret systems shall be permitted exclusively in turret systems that incorporate a mandatory lock-on acquisition period prior to ordnance discharge.

---

## ARTICLE VII — VEHICULAR OPERATIONS AND ARMORED UNITS

**Section 7.01. General Vehicular Requirements.** All armored units, vehicles, mechanized platforms, and analogous conveyances deployed within the territorial boundaries of Ashguard shall satisfy each and all of the following conditions as a prerequisite to lawful operation:

> (a) Full and verified compatibility with the LBA (Listen Based Armor) protocol;
>
> (b) A maximum hover altitude or jetpack-assisted elevation not exceeding fifteen meters (15 m) above the ground surface;
>
> (c) A minimum cooldown period of not less than one (1) minute between successive activations of any self-repair, self-healing, or autonomous damage mitigation function.

### Part B — Vehicular Hit-Point Maxima

**Section 7.02.** The following schedule sets forth the maximum permissible hit-point allocations for each class of vehicle, as classified by the Authority:

| Vehicle Classification | Maximum HP | Armor Protocol |
|:---|:---:|:---:|
| Tanks / Heavy Armored Vehicles | 400 | LBA Standard |
| Gunships / VTOL Platforms | 200 | LBA Standard |
| Fast Attack / Light Vehicles | 100 | LBA Light |
| Fighters / UFO Flight Craft | 10 | LBA Light |

## ARTICLE VIII — SEVERABILITY, ENFORCEMENT, AND GENERAL PROVISIONS

**Section 8.01. Severability.** If any provision, clause, or subsection of this Code, or the application thereof to any person, entity, or circumstance, shall be held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable by any competent authority, such invalidity, illegality, or unenforceability shall not affect any other provision hereof, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect as if the invalid provision had never been incorporated herein.

**Section 8.02. Authority Discretion.** The Authority reserves the unilateral, plenary, and non-reviewable right to interpret, amend, supplement, or modify any provision of this Code at any time and without prior notice. All determinations of the Authority regarding the application, interpretation, or enforcement of this Code shall be final, binding, and conclusive upon all participants.

**Section 8.03. Supersession.** This Code, upon its effective date of publication, hereby supersedes, replaces, and renders null and void all prior versions, drafts, memoranda, oral understandings, or written instruments purporting to govern combat operations within the jurisdiction of Ashguard.
